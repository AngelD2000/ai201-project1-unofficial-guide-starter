SOURCE: Niche — University of Colorado Boulder student reviews
NOTE: One review naming a specific individual with an unverified accusation was excluded during cleaning.

[Junior, 5/5] Boulder is definitely the place to go if you want to be outdoors and never bored. There is always something to do and so many clubs to join. The campus and social life are so much fun. Some of the dorms are a little run-down, and most don't have AC but that's part of the college experience. Off-campus housing can also be a struggle and pretty expensive, but worth it if you find the right place and people to live with.

[Freshman, 5/5] CU Boulder offers beautiful scenery, strong academics, vibrant campus life, and endless outdoor adventures.

[Senior, 3/5] The University of Colorado Boulder has a beautiful campus and is full of wonderful people and opportunities. The biggest challenges I faced were finding those opportunities since it is such a large school.

[Senior, 4/5] CU is a super fun and interesting school. I enjoy the nature and push for health and the outdoors. The people aren't always kind, and the school is overly expensive in each way. Housing in the area is steep unless you have a car and can live further out. It felt similar to high school socially, although that could be a personal experience. Maybe it is the type of people brought in as a school known for parties / being expensive / football.

[Senior, 4/5] Overall, I love the experience at CU Boulder. The campus does a great job getting students involved and the community is great for exploring new interests. Many of the professors are extremely helpful and generous, and a lot of them are great resources for ambitious students looking to get a head start on their careers. Something I would like to see improved is the on-campus housing process — every year it's a huge pain because there are always unexpected changes to the application/assignment process that were never communicated to students. I think need-based housing assignments should be considered rather than "random" assignments.

[Junior, 4/5] The food at the C4C buffet is a really good value, but every other dining hall doesn't provide enough food to feel worth the money. The "entree + 2 sides" system kind of sucks. It counts all drinks as sides, even if the drink is just a cup of water.

[Freshman, 4/5] Beautiful campus with multiple trails, hikes and parks within a short distance. Party life is great and lots of resources to combat unsafe partying.

[Sophomore, 5/5] Fantastic place to be, so many opportunities academically and socially. I was skeptical at first because I thought an in-state public school wouldn't give me the academic opportunities I wanted; however, I have been extremely impressed and had a great time.

[Junior, 5/5] CU Boulder is a wonderful school perfect for people who are socially and physically active. Academics can be strenuous but rewarding; the faculty including professors and TAs are outstanding — passionate, fair, and knowledgeable. The only thing I question is the organization of classes. Some professors constantly change their syllabus or class structure, which is difficult to keep up with alongside other classes. Socially, Boulder is a hotbed of activity with ample nightlife, countless clubs, sports, and meeting places.

[Graduate Student, 4/5] As a working professional, the program is all online which is nice to fit into my schedule. The staff and professors are kind and understanding and the courses are engaging.

[Alum, 4/5] My experience was overall very positive, especially academically. I felt challenged and grew through my coursework, and many professors were knowledgeable and engaging. That said, tuition is very high and doesn't always feel aligned with the support provided. I would like to see more transparency around career outcomes and stronger help with job placement. While resources exist, I often felt like I had to figure things out on my own.

[Alum, 4/5] I had a really fun time at CU Boulder. It is hands down the most beautiful campus in the United States. The city is vibrant and there's so much to do outdoors. Student life is rich and there's always something going on. My only critique would be the lack of diversity.

[Freshman, 1/5] Dorm has mold. Heater in dorm doesn't work. Roommate selection is super haphazard.

[Senior, 4/5] Overall, so thankful for my time here. I found some of my absolute best friends, but I also had to grow a lot. I am so happy with my program in elementary education and having amazing professors.

[Sophomore, 4/5] Academically, the business school curriculum and professors have exceeded expectations. Professors are committed to teaching students and setting them up for successful careers. Dorm housing is alright, but most dorms are very old and not well maintained, and many people have maintenance issues.

[Junior, 5/5] CU Boulder has allowed me to delve into experimental learning with classes focused on applying conceptual understandings to real-world problems. The learning environment is empowering.

[Freshman, 5/5] I love the culture and the academics are very good. The campus life is good and it is easy to get involved.

[Freshman, 5/5] Excellent facilities, services and academic support. The setting of campus at the base of the Colorado Rockies is awesome. The campus is beautiful, well laid out, and easy to navigate on foot. Classrooms are nice, well-lit and welcoming. Food on campus is good with a lot of variety (American, Mexican, Mediterranean, Italian). I've had positive interactions with the academic advisor, registrar, financial aid, and health offices. The teaching staff is excellent and accommodating. My residence hall is nice with many safety features.
